    Basic Emergency Care

    Simple first-aid guidance for common situations while awaiting professional help. In any genuine emergency, please call 112 immediately.

    🫀

    Chest Discomfort

    If someone experiences sudden chest tightness or discomfort that doesn't improve within a few minutes, please seek immediate care.

    • Call 112 right away
    • Have the person sit or lie comfortably
    • Loosen any tight clothing
    • Stay calm and reassure them
    • Do not give food or water
    🌡️

    High Fever

    A fever over 104°F / 40°C, or any fever in a child under 3 months, warrants prompt medical attention.

    • Keep the person hydrated
    • Apply cool (not cold) compress to forehead
    • Remove heavy clothing
    • Paracetamol as directed on package
    • Seek care if fever persists over 3 days
    😮‍💨

    Breathing Difficulty

    If someone is struggling to breathe, acting quickly and calmly is important. Call for help first.

    • Call 112 immediately
    • Help person sit upright — leaning forward slightly
    • Encourage slow, steady breaths
    • Use prescribed inhaler if available
    • Open windows for fresh air
    🤕

    Head Injury

    Any head injury with confusion, vomiting, or loss of consciousness should be evaluated by a doctor promptly.

    • Keep person still and calm
    • Do not move if spinal injury suspected
    • Apply gentle pressure to bleeding wounds
    • Keep them awake and talking
    • Call 112 for significant injuries
    🍽️

    Food-Related Illness

    Vomiting and diarrhea can lead to dehydration. Most cases improve with rest and fluids.

    • Sip water, ORS, or coconut water
    • Rest and avoid solid food initially
    • BRAT diet: banana, rice, applesauce, toast
    • Avoid dairy and spicy foods
    • Seek care if symptoms persist 48+ hours
    🏥

    When to Go to Hospital

    Some situations always need in-person medical evaluation. Trust your instincts — if something feels wrong, please seek help.

    • Difficulty breathing or speaking
    • Uncontrolled bleeding
    • Sudden severe headache
    • Confusion or loss of consciousness
    • Symptoms in infants, elderly, or immunocompromised
